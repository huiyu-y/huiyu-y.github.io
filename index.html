Huiyu

Huiyu Yang is a designer from Taiwan based in Virginia, US. Her design practices as adapting but challenges the existing system in both design and society. She works across various mediums and genres including printed matter, installations, websites and motion pictures. In 2022, she graduated with an MFA from Virginia Commonwealth University. She previously completed a master's in Motion Media Design from Savannah College of Arts and Design.

Inquiries about freelance work or collaborative projects, feel free to reach out to me.

Poster, July 2022. 29.7 x 42 cm

Peace is the soft clouds, the quietness of blue, the moisture of the rain, and the comfort and usual days. Greedy and war covertly hide behind the pacific days that we take for granted. The clouds are the word “peace” spelled in the Morse code. All the dots and dashes are burnt holes of paper. The blue covers the burnt color of the edges. The raindrop graphics cross out the dates of the war. Wars are still occurring after we sign numerous declarations of peace.

Yo-Yo Lin - Object & Method Lecture

Kenetic Poster Design, April 2022.
Loop GIFs, 00:07.

This poster is designed for the Object and Method zoom lecture of Yo-Yo Lin 林友友, a Taiwanese-American, interdisciplinary media artist, at VCUarts Graphic Design program.

This poster was mainly inspired by Yo-Yo's work Re: collections / 再次·回顧 which is based on her return to her motherland in Taiwan during the pandemic and talks about the moment when the artist's memories and experiences across the two lands intersect.The elements of this poster include video clips of ants working, and a kinetic type design of Yo-Yo Lin's name in both English and traditional Chinese(林友友) in a motion that combines and then separates.
